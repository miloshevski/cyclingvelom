History of VELO-M

1990-2000

The history of the club begins with its establishment in 1993 in Kumanovo. The club was founded by brothers Aleksandar and Marjan Petrovski, who were then prominent active cyclists and both held the title of Republic Champions of Macedonia.

Even in its early years, the club achieved significant results. Notably, in 1993, the club organized the International Race - Macedonia '93, which evolved into the Tour of Macedonia in subsequent years. In the first edition of this race, the club won the yellow jersey.

The club also became involved in organizing the October 11 race, which had been held since 1945. By taking over the race, the club actively organized it every year, with almost every edition featuring an international character. In 2018, a record number of cyclists from Macedonia, Serbia, and Bulgaria participated.

In 1994, the club traveled to France in its first year of existence and established a partnership with the French club Louison Bobet in Rennes. There, the club participated in the Race Around Brittany with Marjan Petrovski, Aleksandar Petrovski, Slave Manevski, Panche Shaltevski, and Goran Mishkov.

In its early years, the club actively participated in domestic races as well as on the international stage, including the Tour of Serbia, the Tour of Vojvodina, the Tour of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Belgrade - Budapest, Belgrade - Budapest - Bratislava, the Race Around Pomoravlje, the Grand Prix Gemenc, and the Grand Prix Chepel in Hungary.

Significant results in the first decade were achieved by Marjan and Aleksandar Petrovski in the senior category, while Slave Manevski stood out in the junior category—who later became a Balkan legend and is still remembered in cycling races today. Another notable cyclist who quickly gained recognition on the international scene was Stojche Lazarov from Kochani.

In the national championship, the club earned several titles as the Winner of the Ranking List.

2000-2010

With the start of the new millennium, the club kept pace with significant events in the country. After the end of the conflict in 2001, the club organized a major international cycling race for Peace and Friendship between Macedonia, Albania, and Montenegro. The race went smoothly, with Slave Manevski securing a stage victory, while Veli Sadiki finished third on the stage from Kumanovo to Tetovo.

In the following years, the club strengthened with cyclists from Bulgaria and participated in significant races, including the Balkan Tour, where cyclist Georgi Drumov secured second place, and the Tour of Macedonia, where cyclist Vladimir Koev - Pirana won first place and the yellow jersey.

In 2006, the club participated as a mixed team in the Tour of Turkey, considered one of the leading races in the region, and won the yellow jersey with cyclist Ivailo Gabrovski.

These significant results helped elevate the sport and bring forth new promising cyclists. Coaches Marjan and Aleksandar Petrovski, who ended their careers as athletes, completed their physical education degrees and continued working in the sport as coaches. During this period, successful results were achieved by Stefan Cvetanovski, Srecko Jovchevski, Igor Petkovic, and Sase Velichkovski—who won numerous races in Macedonia and later became the first Macedonian cyclist to join the professional continental team Miche, an Italian-Bulgarian team.

Stefan Petrovski also began his career during this period, quickly rising to prominence from his very first race and remaining a top cyclist to this day. Stefan is considered one of the best cyclists in Macedonian history and holds the most titles as National Champion of Macedonia.

In 2007, Stefan Petrovski participated in the EJF (European Youth Olympic Festival) and later became the first Macedonian cyclist to join the world cycling center – UCI in Aigle, Switzerland, where he continued to build his career. He spent two years there, actively participating in major races in Switzerland, France, Italy, and competing in European and World Championships. While Stefan was competing in Switzerland, significant results were achieved by cyclist Predrag Gelevski, who also made an impact on the international scene, winning a sprint race in Dobrich, Bulgaria, against strong competition.

Upon Stefan's return from Switzerland, the club was built around him, and together with him, it participated in domestic and international races. Notable results were achieved by Aleksandar Atanasovski, Yahi Ibishi, and Predrag Gelevski. During this time, two significant cyclists, Nikola Gjukić and Andrej Petrovski, also began their careers, leaving a lasting mark on Macedonian cycling.

Andrej Petrovski briefly competed in Macedonia in the cadet category before moving to Germany, where he continued to build his career with several different German clubs. He later became the second cyclist to join the world cycling center UCI. Andrej stayed there for three years and achieved historic results that are of great significance for Macedonia and the club Velo-M. From UCI, Andrej continued his career with the Monaco Royal Club, where, during his two seasons, he won a bronze medal at the Olympic Games for small countries representing Monaco.

Andrej Petrovski is a regular and record-holding Macedonian participant in European and World Championships. At the National Championships of Macedonia, Andrej is the National Champion in road cycling for the years 2018, 2019, and 2022, and National Champion in Time Trial for 2018, 2019, 2021, and 2022.

2010-2020

From 2012 to 2016, the club dominated Macedonian cycling, winning most of the races, National Championships, and participating on the international stage in races such as Mersin, Ankara Tour, Serbia Tour, and Belgrade-Banja Luka. During this time, the unbeatable trio in the senior category consisted of Stefan Petrovski, Nikola Gjukić, and Ivica Davidovski, while in the junior category, Denis Kolevski won all races in all disciplines, with Mario Petrovski as the second best.

Predrag Dimevski, who had started his career in the 90s in the cadet category at Velo-M and was living in South Africa, returned to the club. Upon his return, he became the National Champion in Road Cycling in 2013, and again in 2021, representing Velo-M.

In 2016, the club experienced a transition with Stefan Petrovski moving to a German club. At that time, the club was the National Champion on the Ranking List, with key cyclists Nikola Gjukić, Mario Petrovski, Denis Kolevski, Ivica Davidovski, and Ivica Gatzovski. In the upcoming season, Nikola Lefkov and Kiril Markovski joined the club.

2017 is considered one of the most significant years in the club's recent history, as the club expanded and signed sponsorship deals with Asian brands: Ranking – Helmets, Lameda – Equipment, Winspace – Bicycles, and FLR – Shoes, which strengthened the club's position. In 2017, the club participated for the first time in the Cyprus Tour, with the team consisting of Mario Petrovski, Nikola Gjukić, Nikola Lefkov, Ivica Davidovski, Denis Dobrev, and Hristomir Dobrev.

Later that year, the club participated with a six-member team in the Albania Tour UCI 2.2, and some cyclists also competed in the national team of Macedonia in the Belgrade-Banja Luka and Serbia Tour races.

With new legal regulations in 2018, the club partnered with the prominent Kumanovo-based company – Termalift, which specializes in the sale of forklifts and elevators. With the support received, the club was able to build and grow into a Balkan competitor. That year, the club was joined by Andrej Petrovski, who excelled on the Balkan scene and won an international race in Montenegro – the Kuci Trophy, which was held in two stages. Andrej managed to win both stages, earning the yellow jersey, polka-dot jersey, and white jersey. Later, Andrej also secured the title of National Champion in Road Cycling and Time Trial.

In the same year, the club also participated in two races in Bulgaria – Dolchini Cup, and for the second time in the Cyprus Tour.

In 2019, Andrej Petrovski, together with Velo-M, repeated the results from the previous season, becoming National Champion in Road Cycling and Time Trial once again, and winning the Kuci Trophy in Montenegro with even stronger competition, including Jaka Primozic from Slovenia and Dusan Rajovic from Serbia. The club also participated in several races of the Dolchini Cup in Bulgaria that year.

2020-NOW

In 2020, the club underwent significant changes with the emergence of a new generation of cyclists, including Aleksandar Miloshevski, Nemanja Paunovski, Valentino Petkovski, David Georgievski, Jovan Mitev, Leon Miletić, Ivan Ilijevski, and many others. Despite the pandemic, these new cyclists took up the sport, with some developing a passion for it and achieving considerable success. The club also signed several important sponsorship deals, including with Kipeo – a company that produces equipment and continues to support the club. In addition to Kipeo, the club was sponsored by Yaban chains, Santik, and Sidebike shoes.

This generation of Velo-M is one of the most significant in the club's history, and the results speak for themselves. From 2020 to 2023, the club excelled in the categories of cadets, juniors, and seniors across all disciplines, including road, mountain, and cyclocross cycling. The cyclists won a significant portion of the National Championships in all disciplines.

In 2021, the club became the one with the most trophies, totaling around 15. The same year, the club won the Albania Tour and became the first Macedonian club to win the yellow jersey (Dushan Veselinovic) in a race of such a rank. The team consisted of Dushan Veselinovic, Gregor Cerny Matija, Aleksandar Miloshevski, Jovan Mitev, Stevan Klisuric, with Marjan Petrovski and Mario Petrovski leading the team.

Notable results from this period include second place in the Dolchini Cup – Bulgaria (Nemanja Paunovski), third place in the Dolchini Cup (Valentino Petkovski), third place in a race in Vojvodina (Valentino Petkovski), and third place in the 2023 Balkan Championship in mountain biking in Romania (Aleksandar Miloshevski).

In 2024, the club celebrates its 31st anniversary, and it's challenging to summarize everything in just a few sentences. We take this opportunity to thank everyone who has been part of Velo-M from its inception to today. We continue forward!

Breaking points in our history

1993:Founding of Velo-M in Kumanovo by Aleksandar and Marjan Petrovski.

1993:Organization of International Race - Macedonia '93, evolving into the Tour of Macedonia.

1994:Partnership with French club Louison Bobet and participation in Race Around Brittany.

2001:Involvement in organizing a major international race for Peace and Friendship.

2006:Participation in the Tour of Turkey and winning the yellow jersey.

2007:Stefan Petrovski joins UCI World Cycling Center; Predrag Gelevski wins sprint race in Dobrich.

2016:Significant transition with Stefan Petrovski moving to a German club; new cyclists join.

2024:Celebrating 31 years of Velo-M.
